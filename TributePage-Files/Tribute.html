Biography

Steve Jobs (1955-2011) was an American business magnate, inventor, and investor. He was the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple; the chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar; a member of The Walt Disney Company's board of directors following its acquisition of Pixar; and the founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT. He was a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with his early business partner and fellow Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak. Jobs was born in San Francisco and adopted by a couple from Mountain View, California. He dropped out of college after one semester, but remained passionate about electronics and technology. In 1976, he and Wozniak co-founded Apple in Jobs' garage. The company's first product, the Apple I, was a success, and the Apple II, released in 1977, was one of the first mass-produced microcomputers. Jobs was known for his visionary leadership and his perfectionism. He was also a demanding boss, but he was also respected by his employees for his intelligence and his passion for innovation. Under Jobs' leadership, Apple went on to release a string of successful products, including the Macintosh computer, the iPod, the iPhone, and the iPad. Jobs died in 2011 at the age of 56 after a long battle with pancreatic cancer. He is considered one of the most influential figures in the history of technology. Short and sweet: Steve Jobs was a visionary entrepreneur who co-founded Apple and revolutionized the personal computer, music, and mobile phone industries. He was known for his perfectionism and his relentless pursuit of innovation.
